JERRY (Light Blue)
  Action   2 = "Shuffle with full compliance"
  Dialogue 4 = "Rapid and efficient"
  Size       = medium
  Side       = bread
  Why bread? Safe. Predictable. That is Jerry.
  Why medium? Never too much, never too little.
  Why rapid? The Soup Nazi respects zero small talk.

ELAINE (Pink)
  Action   5 = "Walk straight to counter"
  Dialogue 5 = "Brisk and businesslike"
  Size       = large
  Side       = crackers
  Why crackers? She refuses to take bread just because everyone else does.
  Why large? She is not here to be modest.
  Why brisk? The only tone the Soup Nazi tolerates from Elaine.

GEORGE (Yellow)
  Action   3 = "Rehearse order under his breath"
  Dialogue 4 = "Whispering the order"
  Size       = small
  Side       = crackers
  Why crackers? He saw Elaine do it once and has copied her ever since.
  Why small? He is cheap and slightly ashamed of how much he wants this.
  Why whispering? He has rehearsed it so many times it comes out quiet.

KRAMER (Green)
  Action   4 = "Enter backwards somehow"
  Dialogue 4 = "Surprisingly soft and sincere"
  Size       = large
  Side       = bread
  Why bread? He did not think about it. He just said bread.
  Why large? Did you even have to ask?
  Why soft and sincere? It stuns the Soup Nazi into compliance.

NEWMAN (Purple)
  Action   5 = "Announce himself formally"
  Dialogue 1 = "Dramatic and operatic"
  Size       = large
  Side       = bread
  Why bread? Newman always takes bread. He has asked about extra bread.
             The answer was no. He takes bread anyway.
  Why large? Newman does not do small. Newman does not do medium.
  Why dramatic? Newman is the only one who wins by being MORE theatrical.
                The Soup Nazi secretly respects someone who loves soup this much.

WARNING AND BAN SYSTEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

  1 mistake  = WARNING #1   (1 red dot next to name in queue)
  2 mistakes = WARNING #2   (2 red dots)
  3 mistakes = BANNED       (removed from queue, no soup, ever)

  What causes a warning:
    - Wrong action number
    - Wrong dialogue number
    - Wrong size for that character
    - Wrong side for that character
    - Order typed so fast it cannot be read
    - Unknown soup name (not on the menu)
    - Taking longer than 25 seconds

  What DOES NOT cause a warning:
    - Ordering any valid soup (tomato, bisque, etc. — all fine)
