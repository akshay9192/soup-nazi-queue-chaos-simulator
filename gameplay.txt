OVERVIEW
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

You are managing the soup queue at the most feared soup shop in New York.
The Soup Nazi has strict rules about HOW you order — not what soup you pick.
Five Seinfeld characters take turns at the counter. Each one has a specific
way they must approach, a specific tone they must use, a required size, and
a required side. Get all four right and they get soup. Get any wrong and they
get a warning. Three warnings and they are banned forever.

THE CORE CONCEPT — WHAT THE SOUP NAZI ACTUALLY JUDGES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Soup Nazi does not care which soup you order. He cares how you order it.

  SOUP     → Free choice. Any of the 8 soups on the menu works.
             Jerry can get tomato. Or bisque. Or french onion.
             The soup is acknowledged on the result screen but never judged.

  SIZE     → Locked. Each character has a size that fits their personality.
             Order the wrong size and you get a warning.

  SIDE     → Locked. Each character has a personality-driven side.
             Newman takes bread. Elaine takes crackers. George copies Elaine.
             Order the wrong side and you get a warning.

  ACTION   → Locked. How the character physically approaches the counter.
             There is one correct approach per character.
             Use the wrong approach and you get a warning.

  DIALOGUE → Locked. The tone of voice.
             There is one correct tone per character.
             Use the wrong tone and you get a warning.

SCREEN BY SCREEN WALKTHROUGH
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SCREEN 1 — TITLE SCREEN
  Three buttons: Start Game, Chaos Mode, Revenge Mode.
  Click Start Game to begin.
  The queue of 5 characters is waiting. You choose who goes first.

SCREEN 2 — CHARACTER SELECT
  Five coloured cards appear. Each card shows the character's name and
  a one-line bio. Click any card to play that character's turn.
  You choose the order you tackle them in. There is no fixed sequence.

SCREEN 3 — PREP SCREEN (15 seconds)
  Before the character reaches the counter, you get 15 seconds to study
  the full menu displayed on the right panel. The Soup Nazi shouts his
  warning. The countdown clock ticks in the top right corner.
  No typing happens here. Just read. Decide. Press ENTER when ready
  or wait for the timer to expire automatically.
  The screen tells you the order format and reminds you:
    - Soup is your free choice
    - Size and side must match this character

SCREEN 4 — ACTION SELECT
  Five buttons appear, numbered 1 to 5. These are the ways the character
  can physically approach the counter. Only one is correct for each
  character. Click your choice. A log line appears at the bottom of the
  screen confirming your pick.

SCREEN 5 — DIALOGUE SELECT
  Five buttons appear, numbered 1 to 5. These are the tones of voice
  the character can use. Only one is correct for each character.
  Click your choice.

SCREEN 6 — ORDER SCREEN (25 seconds)
  An empty green input box appears in the centre of the screen.
  The right panel still shows the full menu for reference.
  Type your order in the format:   size  soup  side
  Example:  medium tomato bread
  Example:  large french onion crackers
  As you type, a live reading appears below the box showing what
  the game has understood so far. When it shows the right size and side
  highlighted in green on the menu panel you know it has read correctly.
  Press ENTER to submit. You have 25 seconds.

SCREEN 7 — RESULT SCREEN
  The full screen shows the outcome:

  SOUP!          → Green header. Character receives soup. Points awarded.
  WARNING #X/3   → Orange header. One mistake. Explanation shown.
  BANNED!        → Red header. Third mistake. Character removed from queue.

  The screen always shows:
    - What the Soup Nazi said (exact quote)
    - Why the decision was made (what was wrong or right)
    - Which soup the character ordered (acknowledged, never judged)
    - Points gained or lost this turn
    - Running total score

  Click CONTINUE to return to the character select screen.

SCREEN 8 — BACK TO CHARACTER SELECT
  The queue on the left panel updates. Served characters show a green tick.
  Banned characters show a red X. Remaining characters are still available.
  Play continues until every character has either received soup or been banned.

SCREEN 9 — END SCREEN
  Shows final score, total soups given, total bans issued.
  Click PLAY AGAIN to restart or QUIT to exit.

THE THREE-PANEL LAYOUT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The game window is divided into three columns:

  LEFT PANEL — The Queue
    Shows all five characters in order. The current character has an
    arrow next to their name. Warning pips (red dots) show how many
    mistakes each character has made. Served characters show green ticks.
    Banned characters show red X marks. Your score is at the bottom.

  CENTRE PANEL — Main Action
    This is where everything happens. The active screen, buttons, input
    box, result messages, and dialogue log all appear here.
    The dialogue log at the bottom records everything the Soup Nazi
    has said and everything that has happened this session.

  RIGHT PANEL — The Menu
    Always visible. Shows all valid sizes, all valid soups, and all
    valid sides. On the order screen, the size and side you have typed
    highlight in green to confirm the game has read them correctly.
    Soup items never highlight because soup is always a free choice.

THE DIALOGUE LOG
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The box at the bottom of the centre panel is the dialogue log.
It records every event in the session. Colour coding:

  Yellow  → Soup Nazi speaking
  Green   → Soup granted
  Orange  → Warning issued
  Red     → Character banned or "No Soup" line
  Grey    → Narrative event or character quip

AUTOPLAY MODES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHAOS MODE
  Accessible from the title screen. All five characters enter at once.
  Nobody follows the rules. Everyone talks over each other. Everyone
  eventually gets banned. The script plays out line by line.
  Press ENTER or click NEXT to advance each line.
  Press S to cycle the speed between slow, normal, and fast.
  19 lines of escalating comedy ending with the Soup Nazi moving to Argentina.

REVENGE MODE
  Accessible from the title screen. Elaine returns with the Soup Nazi's
  complete recipe book. A 12-line scripted confrontation plays out.
  Press ENTER or click NEXT to advance each line.
  Ends with Elaine winning and the Soup Nazi packing his ladle.

TIPS FOR WINNING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. Use the prep screen to recall the character's required size and side.
   The menu is right there. You have 15 seconds. Use them.

2. Pick any soup you enjoy. The soup does not matter at all.
   Focus entirely on size and side.

3. Know the action and dialogue numbers before you click start.
   The cheat sheet has every character's correct numbers.

4. Type slowly and clearly on the order screen. You have 25 seconds.
   Watch the live reading below the box. When it turns green you are correct.

5. If you get a warning, the result screen tells you exactly what was wrong.
   Read it carefully before the next attempt.

6. Newman is the most counterintuitive character. He wins by being MORE
   dramatic, not less. Dialogue 1 is the loudest option. That is correct.

7. George is easy on timing but easy to confuse. He whispers (dialogue 4)
   and takes small. The cheap small size trips people up.

8. Elaine has the hardest combination to remember because both her correct
   choices are the highest numbers. Action 5, Dialogue 5.
